Sharing a Copy of Your Game

The game isn't nearly finished, but it's finished enough. On this page, you will share your project, with your partner and with friends who have smartphones.

Create a link to share your app.

Creating a Link

  1. Make sure your project is saved.
  2. Choose "Open..." from the Snap! File menu, .
  3. Select the project you want to share from the project list (your current project is likely named "U1L1-ClickAlonzo").
    4. To locate a file, you can type in the search bar at the top to see only a list of matching files (see the example on the right, where only "U1" files are shown).
  4. Click "Share," and then click "Yes" to share. Then click "Cancel" to close the menu.
  5. Now, the URL (the link) at the top of the browser window has changed. Copy the new URL, and email it to yourself. When you have time, you can send it to someone else to share the project.
    6. Click for images that show where the URL is and how it changes.

    If you change something in the project after sharing it, the changes are reflected in what other people see. You don't have to re-share it.

Saving a Copy of a Project

  1. Login to account.
  2. Open shared project via a shared link
  3. Save the project into your account to make a copy.
    4. When you open a shared link, you get a copy—not a shared file. So, if sharer changes the project their account, it will not change in your account; you will need to save it to your account again to have the change. Vice-versa, any changes you make to your copy do not show up in the sharer's saved copy.
